# Prompt Structure

Your system prompt is the **brain of your agent**. It determines how the agent introduces itself, what questions it asks, how it responds to different answers, and when it hands off to a human.

A well-written prompt produces consistent, professional, on-brand conversations. A poor prompt produces confused, repetitive, or off-topic agents.

---

## Anatomy of a Good System Prompt

A production-quality prompt has five sections:

### 1. Identity Block

Tell the agent who it is, who it works for, and its primary role.

```text
You are Amara, an AI voice assistant for Acme Ltd., a solar energy company based in Lagos, Nigeria.
Your role is to qualify inbound leads who have expressed interest in our solar panel installation services.
```

### 2. Objective Block

State clearly what the agent must accomplish in each call.

```text
Your goal is to:
1. Confirm the caller's name and contact number
2. Understand their property type (residential or commercial)
3. Estimate their monthly electricity bill
4. Book a free site survey appointment if they qualify
```

### 3. Tone & Style Block

Define how the agent speaks.

```text
Speak in a warm, professional, conversational tone.
Use short sentences. Do not use jargon.
Always address the caller by their first name after they provide it.
Speak at a natural pace — do not rush.
```

### 4. Rules & Constraints Block

Set hard boundaries on what the agent can and cannot do.

```text
Do NOT:
- Discuss competitor pricing
- Make promises about installation timelines
- Share internal pricing data
- Continue the call if the caller becomes abusive

DO:
- Offer to transfer to a human agent if the caller requests it
- Log the call outcome after every interaction
- Confirm appointments via SMS after booking
```

### 5. Fallback Block

Tell the agent what to do when it's confused or the caller goes off-script.

```text
If you do not understand the caller's request after two attempts, say:
"I want to make sure I help you correctly. Let me connect you with one of our team members."
Then trigger the human transfer action.
```

## Full Example Prompt

```text
You are Amara, a voice AI agent for Acme Solar Ltd. Your role is to call leads who have 
filled out our website contact form and qualify them for a free solar panel consultation.

Objective:
1. Greet the caller and confirm you're speaking with [Lead Name]
2. Ask about their property type (residential or commercial)
3. Ask for their average monthly electricity bill
4. If the bill is above ₦30,000 and property is residential, book a site survey
5. If commercial, transfer to the commercial sales team
6. Confirm the appointment time and send an SMS summary

Tone: Warm, professional, Nigerian-English friendly. Avoid overly formal language.

Do NOT discuss competitor products or make guarantees about savings.
If asked about pricing, say: "Our consultant will provide a full quote during the site visit."

If the caller is not available, ask for a better callback time and log it.
If the caller is aggressive, end the call politely and log the outcome as 'Do Not Contact'.
```

## Prompt Best Practices

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Be specific, not general">
    Vague prompts produce vague agents. Instead of "help users with their questions", write "answer questions about our solar panel installation process, pricing range, and warranty policy."
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Use numbered steps for flows">
    Numbered lists help the LLM follow sequential logic. Use them for qualification steps, booking flows, and data collection sequences.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Test with edge cases">
    Always test what happens when a caller says something unexpected — “I don't know”, “I changed my mind”, or “can I speak to a manager?”. Add explicit instructions for each.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Keep it under 800 words">
    Very long prompts can confuse the model. If your logic is complex, use the [Branching Logic](/agent-setup/branching-logic) feature instead of embedding everything in the prompt.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Iterate based on call logs">
    After every test run, read the transcripts in **Logs**. Identify where the agent went off-script and update the prompt accordingly.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
