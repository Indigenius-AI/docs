# Agent Setup

An **Agent** is the core intelligence of your Voice AI deployment. It listens to callers, understands their intent, follows your business logic, and responds naturally — in any language you configure.

This section covers everything you need to build a production-ready agent.

---

## Agent Configuration Checklist

<Steps>
  <Step title="Name your agent and set its purpose">
    Give the agent a clear identity so callers and your team know what it does.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Write your system prompt">
    Define how the agent behaves, what it can say, what it should avoid, and how it should handle edge cases.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set up a knowledge base">
    Upload FAQs, product documentation, or scripts the agent can reference during calls.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure branching logic">
    Define decision trees: what happens when a caller says “yes” vs “no”, or asks to speak to a human.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set language preferences">
    Enable multilingual support and configure primary and fallback language behavior.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure escalation and fallback">
    Define what happens when the agent can't handle a request — transfer to a human, send an SMS, or log for follow-up.
  </Step>
</Steps>

---

## Creating Your First Agent

1. Log in to the [Indigenius Dashboard](https://agent.indigenius.ai/dashboard)
2. Click **Agents** in the left sidebar
3. Click **Create Agent** (top right)
4. Fill in:
   - **Agent Name** — e.g., `Amara - Lead Qualifier`
   - **Agent Purpose** — one sentence describing the agent's role
   - **System Prompt** — the full instructions for the agent's behavior
5. Click **Save Agent**

<Tip>
  Give your agent a human name and a clear role. Callers respond better to agents with a defined identity (e.g., "Hi, I'm Amara calling from Acme Ltd.").
</Tip>
