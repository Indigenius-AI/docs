# Branching Logic

**Branching Logic** lets you define conditional paths in your agent's conversation flow — so the agent says different things depending on what the caller says.

This is how you build structured workflows like lead qualification funnels, appointment booking flows, and survey sequences.

---

## How Branching Works

A branch is a decision point in the conversation. At each branch:

1. The agent asks a question or makes a statement
2. The caller responds
3. The agent routes to the next step based on the response

```text
[Start]
  ↓
Agent: "Are you the property owner?"
  ├─ Yes → Ask about electricity bill
  ├─ No  → Ask for owner's contact
  └─ Not sure → Explain eligibility and ask again
```

---

## Configuring Branches

1. Go to **Agents →** select your agent **→ Workflow** tab
2. Click **Add Step**
3. Choose a **Step Type**:
   - **Ask** — agent asks a question and waits for a response
   - **State** — agent makes a statement (no expected response)
   - **Transfer** — agent transfers the call to a phone number or team
   - **End** — agent ends the call
4. Add the **Response Conditions** (e.g., "if caller says yes", "if caller mentions a price above X")
5. Connect each condition to the next step
6. Save your workflow

---

## Example: Lead Qualification Branch

```text
Step 1 - Greeting
  Agent: "Hello, this is Amara from Acme Solar. Am I speaking with [Name]?"
  ├─ Yes → Step 2
  └─ No  → Apologise and End Call

Step 2 - Property Type
  Agent: "Great! Is your property residential or commercial?"
  ├─ Residential → Step 3A
  └─ Commercial   → Step 3B

Step 3A - Residential Bill
  Agent: "What is your average monthly electricity bill?"
  ├─ Above ₦30k → Book Appointment (Step 4)
  └─ Below ₦30k → Not Qualified (Step 5)

Step 3B - Commercial Transfer
  Agent: "Let me connect you with our commercial team."
  → Transfer to +234-XXX-XXXX

Step 4 - Book Appointment
  Agent: "I'd love to schedule a free site survey for you..."
  → Collect date/time → Confirm via SMS → End Call

Step 5 - Not Qualified
  Agent: "Thank you for your time. We'll be in touch when we have plans that suit your usage."
  → End Call
```

---

## Best Practices

- **Keep branches shallow.** Aim for no more than 5 decision levels deep. Deep trees are hard to maintain and confuse callers.
- **Handle "I don't know" responses.** Always add a fallback branch for vague or unclear answers.
- **Test every path.** Walk through every branch manually before going live. See [Testing Checklist](/testing/checklist).
- **Use the prompt for nuance.** Branching logic handles the structure; your system prompt handles tone and style.
