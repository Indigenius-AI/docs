# Escalation & Fallback

No AI agent can handle every situation perfectly. **Escalation** is what happens when a caller needs human help. **Fallback** is what happens when the agent doesn’t understand. Configuring both correctly is essential to a high-quality caller experience.

---

## Escalation Paths

You can configure up to three escalation options per agent:

### 1. Transfer to a Human Agent

The call is forwarded to a live phone number (your support team, sales team, etc.).

**Setup:**

1. Go to **Agents →** your agent **→ Escalation** tab
2. Enable **Human Transfer**
3. Enter the **Transfer Phone Number** (must be a verified number)
4. Set a **Transfer Message** — what the agent says before connecting
   > _"Let me connect you with one of our team members. Please hold for a moment."_
5. Define **Trigger Conditions** — when should the agent escalate?
   - Caller explicitly asks for a human
   - Agent fails to understand 2\+ times in a row
   - Caller uses frustrated or abusive language
   - Specific keywords detected (e.g., "cancel", "legal action", "complaint")

### 2. Schedule a Callback

Instead of an immediate transfer, the agent books a callback for a later time.

**Setup:**

1. Enable **Callback Scheduling** in the Escalation tab
2. Connect to your [CRM Integration](/crm-integration/overview) to log the callback request
3. Set the **Callback Window** — hours when your team is available
4. Configure the SMS confirmation message

### 3. Send an SMS or Email Follow-Up

The agent ends the call and sends a follow-up message with a link or contact details.

**Setup:**

1. Enable **SMS Follow-Up** or **Email Follow-Up**
2. Compose your follow-up message template
3. Use variables like `{{caller_name}}`, `{{agent_name}}`, `{{date}}`

## Fallback Handling

Fallback triggers when the agent genuinely doesn’t understand the caller.

### Configure Your Fallback Response

Add this to your system prompt:

```text
If you do not understand the caller's request:
- First attempt: Politely ask them to rephrase. Say: "I'm sorry, I didn't quite catch that. Could you say that again?"
- Second attempt: Try a different interpretation and ask for confirmation.
- Third attempt: Escalate. Say: "I want to make sure you get the right help. Let me connect you with a team member."
Never leave the caller in silence for more than 3 seconds.
```

### Silence Handling

If the caller says nothing for more than 4 seconds:

```text
If the caller is silent for more than 4 seconds, say:
"I'm still here — take your time. How can I help you today?"
If silence continues for another 5 seconds, say:
"I'll go ahead and end the call for now. Feel free to call us back anytime. Goodbye!"
Then end the call.
```

---

## Fallback Best Practices

- **Never let the agent say "I don't know" without an alternative.** Always pair uncertainty with an action: transfer, callback, or follow-up.
- **Log every fallback event.** Review your [call logs](/testing/checklist) to identify recurring fallback triggers. They reveal gaps in your prompt or knowledge base.
- **Keep transfer numbers live.** A transfer to a disconnected number is worse than no escalation. Test your transfer numbers weekly.
